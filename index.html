// ABOUT

Incubating the Future

Platinum Venture Labs was created to incubate and accelerate breakthrough ideas from the next generation of entrepreneurs. We identify, support, and invest in high-potential ventures led by exceptional early-stage founders.

Backed by the Poyiadjis Family Trust and extending the two-decade legacy of Platinum Capital Partners, PVL provides more than just capital. We embed ourselves alongside founders—providing strategic direction, operational expertise, and direct introductions to the family offices, institutional investors, and industry leaders who co-invest with us. Our model is built on conviction: fewer bets, deeper partnerships, and a long-term commitment to building companies that matter.

Strategic Guidance

Hands-on mentorship and strategic direction from seasoned investors with 20+ years of cross-sector experience.

Growth Capital

Flexible capital deployment tailored to each venture's stage and trajectory, from seed to scale.

Network Access

Direct access to family offices, institutional investors, and industry leaders across technology, energy, and defense.

Our Approach

01

Identify

We source exceptional founders working on frontier problems in AI, cybersecurity, and autonomous systems—filtering for technical depth, market timing, and founder conviction.

02

// OUR HERITAGE

Built on Two Decades of Proven Investment

Platinum Venture Labs is a subsidiary of Platinum Capital Partners Inc. (PCP), a holding company for the Poyiadjis Family Trust founded in 2005 by Roys Poyiadjis. Over the last 20 years, PCP has completed approximately 65 investment transactions across technology, energy infrastructure, real estate, private equity, and special situations—co-investing alongside family offices, institutional investors, and global industry leaders.

PVL extends this legacy into frontier technology, channeling two decades of cross-sector deal-making experience into backing the next generation of founders.

Biofuel · Energy Infrastructure Operating

Kashima Biofuel Power Plant

Kashima Port, 71 miles from Tokyo, Japan

The largest biofuel power plant in Japan. Platinum was the project sponsor, originally acquiring the power plant to be disassembled and relocated to Cyprus to provide power following an explosion that destroyed the country's largest plant. After the Cyprus Euro Crisis, the decision was made to switch the fuel source to biofuel and build in Japan instead.

JV Partner Family office of Martua Sitorus, CEO of Wilmar International—the world's largest palm oil producer (~$65B revenue). Wilmar served as the fuel partner providing consistent palm oil supply.
Economics Following the Fukushima earthquake, Japan offered generous feed-in tariffs for renewable energy. The Kashima plant feed-in tariff is valued at $1.5 billion over 20 years.
$1.5B Feed-in Tariff, 20-Year Contract JV with Wilmar International
Natural Gas · Energy Infrastructure Development

Puerto Rico Natural Gas Pipeline

Decentricity LLC (PCP Subsidiary)

Decentricity LLC was founded for the purpose of "gasifying" Puerto Rico. The company holds an exclusive franchise granted by the Puerto Rico government to operate a natural gas pipeline, tied to an existing right of way previously used by Shell to transport petroleum products.

Infrastructure Delivers natural gas from either the south (LNG import terminal in Peñuelas) or north side of Puerto Rico to all major PREPA power plants: Costa Sur, Aguirre, Palo Seco, and San Juan.
Economics Project capital costs expected at $600M. Annual fuel savings of $400M by switching from diesel to natural gas across the island's power generation fleet.
$600M Capital · $400M Annual Savings Exclusive Government Franchise

Real Estate · Historic Redevelopment Redevelopment

Cincinnati Terrace Plaza

600,000 sq ft Mixed-Use, Downtown Cincinnati

Built in 1948, the 24-story Terrace Plaza hotel was designed by architecture firm Skidmore, Owings & Merrill—the same firm behind the Burj Khalifa and One World Trade Center. The Modernist building received national attention for its design and originally featured space for JC Penney, Bond department stores, and a 324-room hotel.

Redevelopment 162 luxury residential apartment units, refurbished 262-room hotel (previously Hilton and Crowne Plaza), plus retail and office renovation across all components.
Financing $75M Public-Private Partnership with the City of Cincinnati including Federal and State tax credits, TIF (Tax Increment Financing), grants, and other tax abatements.
